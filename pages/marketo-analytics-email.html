Delivery & Health
Before engagement, there's delivery.

A message that doesn't arrive can't be read. Hard bounces are permanent delivery failures — the address doesn't exist or the domain has rejected the message outright. Marketo suppresses these automatically, so a climbing hard bounce rate is usually a sign that recent acquisition brought in lower-quality contacts or that the list hasn't been refreshed in a while. Soft bounces are temporary — a full mailbox, an overloaded mail server, or a transient network issue. Marketo will retry soft bounces automatically over the following days, so a single elevated spike is usually self-correcting. If the same address soft-bounces repeatedly across multiple sends, Marketo will eventually convert it to a hard bounce. Reputation metrics — complaints and unsubscribes — are covered in their own section below.

First Impressions
The open is won
before the email is opened.

Your audience decides in under two seconds — based on sender name, subject line, and the time the email landed in their inbox. Open rate is the clearest signal of how well those three elements align with your audience's expectations. The day-of-week and hour breakdowns reveal your programme's natural rhythm; the heatmap shows where sends and opens cluster together. When open rate is low, look at subject lines and sender address before assuming a content problem.

Reputation
Sender reputation is cumulative
and slow to recover.

Unsubscribes are healthy — they keep your active list clean and reflect honest intent from someone who's moved on. Complaints are a different matter: even a small cluster can trigger inbox filtering at major providers, and that filtering affects future sends to everyone on that domain, not just the person who complained. The watchlist below surfaces individual sends sitting above normal thresholds. Investigate the audience and content before sending to that segment again — a poorly-targeted send that looks fine in aggregate can quietly erode your deliverability.

Metric definitions Hard Bounce — Permanent delivery failure (invalid/non-existent address, domain does not exist). Marketo suppresses the address automatically. High rates indicate list quality issues.
Soft Bounce — Temporary failure (mailbox full, server unavailable, message too large). Marketo retries automatically; persistent soft bounces are eventually treated as hard bounces.
Unsubscribe — Recipient opted out via the email's unsubscribe link. Marketo marks them as Marketing Suspended. Normal threshold: < 0.5% of delivered.
Complaint — Recipient clicked "Mark as spam" in their mail client (reported via FBL). Even 1–2 complaints signals a deliverability risk; high complaint rates can damage sender reputation and trigger ISP filtering.
